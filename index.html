TRACK RUNS

FunRun allows runners to track and view information about their runs, including statistics such as speed, distance, and duration. The app automatically tracks the user's progress over time, making it easy to understand improvements.

HISTORY RUNS

FunRun keeps a record of all runs, allowing users to view statistics from individual runs or collections of runs. This feature enables runners to monitor their progress and understand how far they have come since they first started using the app.

GOAL ACHIEVING

FunRun provides a platform for runners to set and follow their own personalized running goals. The app encourages users to achieve their goals by keeping track of their progress. The app's goal and training plan systems are scalable, allowing runners of all abilities to set achievable challenges.

UNIQUE DASHBOARD

FunRun's dashboard is designed to be user-friendly, making it easy for runners to access their information quickly. The dashboard shows historical data in day, week and month, allowing users to monitor their progress easily. The app also allows multiple runners to use the same device to track their runs by selecting a different user, making it accessible to a wider demographic.


VISION STATEMENT


The FunRun application will be used by runners to track and view information about their runs. It also offers a platform for runners to set and follow their running goals, and to compare their progress against their historical runs.

The goals set by users within FunRun will be about trackable statistics, such as speed, distance, and duration. These statistics, and by extension the user’s progress, will all be automatically tracked by FunRun. Runners will be reminded of their goals and encouraged to achieve them. In addition, Users will be able to view statistics from individual runs, or from collections of runs, and understand the improvements that they have made since the first time they used FunRun even in the absence of goals.

The design is intended to be friendly to new runners and experienced runners alike. FunRun’s goal and training plan systems are able to scale alongside runners as they progress, so there is always a new challenge. Additionally, multiple runners may use the same device to track their runs by selecting a different user. This allows anyone, regardless of age or ability, to try FunRun without worrying about disturbing someone else’s progress.

This application is intended to be used on mobile Android devices, however it is planned to be extensible to allow future versions to operate on smart watches.

FunRun is a necessary advancement in exercise technology. In modern times, many people have become stagnant, and by introducing a way to make exercise more enjoyable, FunRun is able appeal and stay engaging to a wider demographic. Additionally, running is a very accessible type of exercise, since little equipment is necessary, further increasing the need for such an application. Viewing the progress made over time will give users the joy of achievement. Overall, FunRun aims to make running more enjoyable, and to make it easy to incorporate running into one’s routine.

POSTMORTEM



How did the project change from your initial (iteration 0) vision or stories, or did it work out as predicted?

During the development process, the main structure of the FunRun project remained largely unchanged from the initial iteration 0 vision and stories. However, the team had planned additional features such as sharing progress through images or other apps. While this feature was not ultimately included in the final product, the team is pleased with how the project turned out and believes it achieved its goal of making running more enjoyable and accessible to a wider demographic.

What did you learn about team or large project development? What will you start doing, keep doing, or stop doing next time?

During the development of the FunRun application, our team learned a lot about effective team and large project development. We found that GitLab was a great tool for collaboration, version control, and issue tracking, and we plan to continue using it in future projects. Additionally, we learned the importance of creating a clear architecture diagram and breaking down the project into manageable user stories with assigned issues to team members. This allowed for more efficient development and ensured that everyone on the team was on the same page. Overall, we will continue to prioritize effective communication, planning, and documentation in future team projects.

Can you draw any conclusions from what you’ve done?

After completing the FunRun project, we can draw several conclusions about what we've done. Firstly, we learned that effective communication is essential for successful teamwork. Without clear communication, team members may end up working on different parts of the project or may not be aware of changes made by others, leading to confusion and delays.
We also found that version control, specifically GitLab, is an excellent tool for managing and tracking changes to the project's codebase. It provides a centralized repository for all team members to access and update the code, making it easy to collaborate and keep track of changes made by each team member.
Furthermore, we realized that organizing the code into different layers or components is crucial to keep the codebase manageable and maintainable. This separation of concerns allows for easier debugging, testing, and maintenance, making the codebase more scalable and efficient in the long run.
In conclusion, the FunRun project has taught us the importance of effective communication, version control, and proper code organization. We plan to apply these lessons in future large-scale projects to ensure a smoother development process.
